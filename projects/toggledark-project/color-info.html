Role of Each Color

Primary: Use these for the most important actions or information. They are bold and eye-catching, driving user focus.

Secondary: Use these for secondary actions or supporting information. They complement the primary color but have a less dominant visual impact.

Accents: These colors are for alerts, notifications, or elements you want to stand out. They create contrast without overwhelming the design.

Design Tips

For light themes, lighter backgrounds (like gray-100) with darker text (like gray-800) provide excellent readability.

For dark themes, avoid using pure black or white to reduce eye strain; softer shades like gray-900 and gray-200 offer a more comfortable viewing experience.
